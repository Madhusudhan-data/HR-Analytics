# 📊 HR Analytics Dashboard

An interactive **HR Analytics Dashboard built with Power BI** to analyze employee attrition, workforce demographics, salary distribution, job roles, and employee trends.

This project transforms raw HR data into meaningful business insights that help HR teams understand **employee turnover patterns and workforce behavior**.

## 🚀 Project Overview

Employee attrition is a major challenge for organizations. This project focuses on analyzing HR data to identify key factors influencing employee attrition.

The dashboard provides a comprehensive view of:

- 👥 Employee workforce overview
- 📉 Employee attrition analysis
- 💼 Attrition by job role
- 💰 Salary-wise attrition
- 🎓 Education field analysis
- 👨‍💼 Gender-based attrition
- 🎂 Age-wise attrition
- 🏢 Years at company analysis

The dashboard is designed to support **data-driven HR decision-making**.

## 📊 Dashboard Insights

### 🔹 Attrition by Education Field
- **Life Sciences** employees represent the highest share of attrition.
- **Medical** employees contribute significantly to overall attrition.
- Marketing and Technical Degree employees show comparatively lower attrition levels.

### 🔹 Attrition by Age
- Employees aged **26–35** have the highest attrition.
- Younger employees in the **18–25** age group also show noticeable attrition.
- Attrition decreases significantly among employees aged **55+**.

### 🔹 Attrition by Salary
- Employees earning **up to 5K** show the highest attrition.
- Attrition decreases as salary ranges increase.
- This highlights the importance of analyzing **compensation and employee retention**.

### 🔹 Attrition by Job Role
- **Laboratory Technicians** have the highest attrition.
- **Sales Executives** and **Research Scientists** also contribute significantly to employee turnover.

### 🔹 Attrition by Gender
- Male employees account for a higher number of attrition cases than female employees.

### 🔹 Attrition by Years at Company
- Employees with fewer years of experience show higher attrition.
- Attrition is particularly high during the early stages of an employee's career.
- This indicates the importance of **employee engagement and retention strategies**.
